RMNCAH - Birth still fresh Stillbirths fresh RMNCAH_SBF Still birth is defined as infant born with no signs of life, weighting more than 1,000 g and older than 28 weeks gestation. An antepartum foetal death (stillbirth – macerated) refers to a foetus that has suffered an intrauterine death after the 28th week of gestation and before labour. An intrapartum foetal death (stillbirth - fresh), refers to a baby that has died after the onset of labour and before birth. Fresh stillbirths do not show any signs of maceration. Maceration describes the degenerative changes that occur in stillbirths retained in the utero after death, and the earliest signs are in the form of discolouration and peeling of the skin, leaving regions of raw tissue.
